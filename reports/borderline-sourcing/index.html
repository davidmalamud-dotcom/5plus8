Segment Read

Borderline should not build one sourcing list. It should build parallel lists because each segment sees different kinds of inventory at different moments.

Commercial Fitness Distributors

Best source for trade-ins, replacement cycles, and facilities refreshing entire packages. These teams often know what is leaving before the market does.

Fitness Logistics and Installation

Strong source for relocation, extraction, and reinstallation opportunities. These operators are closest to equipment moving in and out of service.

Commercial Movers

Best source for office gyms, amenity spaces, and corporate facilities during closures, consolidations, and redesigns.

Corporate Wellness Operators

Useful for campus, tenant, and employee fitness centers where they influence procurement, refreshes, or vendor relationships nationally.

Trade-In and Amenity Specialists

These groups see refresh cycles in multifamily, hospitality, and corporate environments and often have a cleaner line of sight into outgoing equipment.

Hospitality Fitness Distributors

Hotel and resort fitness specialists who see refresh cycles before the broader market does. When a brand upgrades a fitness room, these are the first calls. Trade-in coordination and extraction are core parts of the relationship.

Other Avenues

Some of the best opportunities will show up outside direct company outreach. These are the side channels Borderline should monitor if it wants earlier visibility into closures, liquidations, and refresh cycles.

Trade-In and Buyback Pages

  • 360 Fitness Solutions
  • Gym Store trade-in programs
  • PreOwnedGym and similar upgrade-credit funnels

Auction and Liquidation Channels

  • Local liquidators and surplus resellers
  • HiBid, Proxibid, AuctionZip, and regional auction houses
  • Search terms like gym liquidation, fitness center closeout, and office gym auction

Informal Signal Sources

  • Facebook Marketplace and Craigslist for small and mid-size operators
  • Reddit and reseller communities as signal channels, not primary contact databases
  • Local broker and move-manager referrals where office gyms are being decommissioned
